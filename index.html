GETTING HERE

Flying:
The closest airport, about an hour away, is Bradley International Airport in Hartford, CT. If you don't rent a car, you can reserve a shuttle with Valley Transporter to get to Amherst. Logan International Airport in Boston is about 2 hours away by car.

Train:
The closest Amtrak station is in Northampton.

Bus:
Megabus has a route from Manhattan to Hadley, MA (very close to the Holiday Inn Express), and the Peter Pan Bus travels from Manhattan to Northampton.

The wedding is over Memorial Day weekend, and it's also reunion weekend for Amherst College, so please don't delay in reserving a room!

We've booked a block of rooms under "Anzaroot-Morgenstern Wedding" at the Holiday Inn Express in Hadley, MA. Please call (413) 582-0002 to reserve your room. (You can also book online here) For Friday and Saturday night, the rate is $192/night; for Sunday night, the rate is $122. (Those rates are for two queen beds or one king bed.)

The Holiday Inn Express is near many shops, grocery stores, a movie theater, and more, and it's about a 10-minute drive from the Yiddish Book Center. However, you really can't walk anywhere from the hotel, so you should consider bringing/renting a car if you stay there.

There are many other options for hotels in the area. One that we'd recommend is the Hotel Northampton, which is a little pricier but right in the heart of downtown Northampton. It is walkable to shops and restaurants, as well as Smith College and its museums.

There are a few cute B&Bs in downtown Amherst, as well as the Lord Jeffery Inn and the Hotel UMass. Amherst is smaller than Northampton but it's also very fun and walkable with shops, restaurants, and museums.

ABOUT PIONEER VALLEY

The Pioneer Valley is a popular, year-round tourist destination. Travelers are drawn to the Pioneer Valley by its lively college towns, such as Northampton and Amherst; its unspoiled nature, numerous parks, and recreational facilities, including New England's largest and most popular amusement park, Six Flags New England in Agawam; its cultural and historical sites, such as the Emily Dickinson House in Amherst, the Springfield Armory National Historical Site, and the Basketball Hall of Fame on Springfield's riverfront.

ABOUT THE YIDDISH BOOK CENTER

The Yiddish Book Center is a nonprofit organization working to tell the whole Jewish story by rescuing, translating, and disseminating Yiddish books and presenting innovative educational programs that broaden understanding of modern Jewish identity.
